Sally Grace Contarino

26 years old  ·  Melbourne, Victoria, Australia  ·  5'5", ~130 lbs, fair skin  ·  Brown hair  ·  Brown eyes  ·  Slim build

Timeline — Before She Disappeared

  • Sally, a frequent traveller to the US, was on a solo holiday that had already included stops in Palm Springs, California, before she arrived in the Las Vegas area.
  • June 17, 3:46 PM: Newly surfaced footage — recorded on an influencer's Meta smart glasses and shared publicly on June 28 — appears to show Sally outside the 7-Eleven, 4880 Boulder Highway, Las Vegas. In the clip a man approaches her and asks for her number. She tells him she has a boyfriend. He apologizes and asks if she's waiting for her boyfriend, to which she replies, "No, I'm just gonna have a cigarette and go inside." He then gets in his car and leaves. Police say they have no indication the man is connected to her disappearance and confirm the footage was provided to them before it was posted online.
  • June 19, ~8:00 PM: Sally texted friends: "I'm about to hike up Lone Mountain then go home — I fly back tomorrow."
  • June 19, 8:45–10:28 PM: CCTV placed Sally at Timbers Bar & Grill, a locals bar near UNLV. She arrived on foot, sat at the bar, had drinks, played gaming machines, spoke with other patrons, and used an ATM. Staff, including manager Jocelyn Zeigler-Arthur, described her as "quiet" and appearing calm, content, and "not under any stress." She left alone around 10:28 PM, walking eastbound on Cheyenne Street.
  • Early hours, June 20: CCTV captured Sally returning to The Bungalows Hostel in downtown Las Vegas (an area some locals describe as high-crime at night). There is no known CCTV of her leaving the hostel again.
  • June 20 morning: Property staff confirmed Sally checked out remotely via the hostel's website/app and left her luggage behind, saying she'd return for it. She then visited a nearby café she regularly used in Las Vegas, ordering breakfast and a protein smoothie.
  • June 20, ~1:02 PM: CCTV captured Sally leaving Smith's Marketplace in Las Vegas. Police believe she then boarded a local bus and was last seen near 9700 W. Skye Canyon Drive, consistent with the Mount Charleston / Lone Mountain corridor northwest of the Strip. Investigators believe she got off and began a hike in the area — a hike she had reportedly done before — but she never checked in at the Mount Charleston Visitor Center and was never seen returning.
  • June 20, ~1100 GMT-7 (approx.): Last contact with her family in Australia.
  • A Greyhound bus ticket she had purchased for Las Vegas → Los Angeles — her normal route back to catch her international flight — was never scanned. No CCTV shows her boarding that bus, and she was not sighted in the vicinity of the terminal.
  • June 21: Sally failed to board her scheduled international flight home to Melbourne. Her family reported her missing shortly after.

Investigation & Search Efforts Updated

  • LVMPD has opened a missing persons case and classified Sally as an "endangered missing adult" who "might possibly be in severe emotional distress and in need of medical assistance."
  • Police have recovered Sally's luggage from the hostel.
  • Search dogs (K9 units) have been deployed using her clothing to try to pick up her scent, alongside a grid search of the Lone Mountain / Mount Charleston area. Sniffer dogs have followed the tourist's scent from Smith's Marketplace to an area several kilometres away closer to the edge of town.
  • Red Rock Search & Rescue is assisting LVMPD and has publicly appealed for sightings, noting Sally's trip reportedly included stops in Las Vegas, Mount Charleston, and Southern California.
  • Hospitals across the Nevada region have been contacted and asked to check patient records in case Sally sought treatment under a name that wasn't immediately flagged.
  • Online sleuthing communities (including Websleuths, where Sally's family has been posting directly) have called for a focused search of Lone Mountain — specifically the summit scramble, east-side gullies, off-trail washes, and desert perimeter — along with helicopter and drone thermal passes at dawn/dusk and a review of trailhead car-park camera footage from June 20.
  • Sally's two sisters have travelled from Australia to Las Vegas to assist authorities. One sister has been acting as the family's point of contact with police in both Australia and the US; a second sister travelled to the US to be on the ground. A source described them as "exhausted" but "working alongside authorities."
  • Correction/clarification: The Mount Charleston photo included in LVMPD's official missing person bulletin was taken during a previous trip to Las Vegas, not her most recent one — it was used because it was the most recent clear photo the family had on hand, and has since been widely (and sometimes confusingly) shared as if from her final trip.
  • As of this update, Sally has not been located. The search is entering its third week.
